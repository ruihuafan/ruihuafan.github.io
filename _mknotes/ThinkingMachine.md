# Towards a thinking machine

Out of curiosity, I read this famous paper by Turing, Computing Machinery and Intelligence, which is really a humbling experience. 

The start of the paper is already shocking to me, "I propose to consider the question, `Can machines think?'". It is hard for me to imagine how Turing proposed such a question and then provided extensive critical (although fairly philosophical and thus entertaining) thinkings of the question itself and possible solutions. He also envisioned that a thinking machine will be possible in about 50 years. I am sure he would be fairly happy if he could see what is happening right now. 

Nowadays, most of his concerns, e.g., the feasibility of having powerful digitial computers, are no longer necessary. Still, I found it inspiring and interesting to revisit his discussion for the sake of better educating myself. Below, I will mention a few of his points and provide my own thoughts.
